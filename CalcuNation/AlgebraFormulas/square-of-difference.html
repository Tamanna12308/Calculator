Step-by-Step Solution

(a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b²

    Description of the Formula

    The square of the difference formula states that when you square the difference of two numbers, the result is the sum of their squares minus twice their product.

    Formula History

    Historically, the Babylonians used the difference of two squares to calculate multiplications.

    Practice Problems

    Problem 1: Find the expansion of (7 - 4)².

    (7 - 4)² = 7² - 2(7×4) + 4² = 49 - 56 + 16 = 9

    Problem 2: If a = 10 and b = 6, find (a - b)².

    (10 - 6)² = 10² - 2(10×6) + 6² = 100 - 120 + 36 = 16
